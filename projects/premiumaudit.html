Premium Audit

Helps customers complete a required audit for premiums paid on their Insurance Policy

About the Project

This portfolio piece is based on a project I worked on for Employers Insurance Group (NYSE:EIG). Employers is a Worker's Compensation Insurance Carrier for small businesses in the United States.

Project Timeline

April 2019

August 2019

Interesting Facts

The project team used Agile Scrum Methodology through the project lifecyle. I was the sole User Experience Designer, and participated in all scrum ceremonies and workshops.

The project team included approximately 10 team members across 3 U.S. timezones. I worked both remotely and from the company's office in Kansas City, MO.

My Role

  • User Research & Usability Testing
  • User Experience & User Interface Design
  • Contributed to HTML & CSS within Angular v8.0+
  • Created & Maintained a Design System
  • Contributed to a UI Component Library

Lola

The Yoga Studio Owner

Lola owns a yoga and meditation studio with 2 full-time employees and 7 part-time employees.

Lola runs the yoga and meditation studio and teaches several of the classes throughout the day.

Goals

Lola wishes she could teach more classes, instead of spending so much time managing her business.

Expectations

Lola is tech-savvy, but she expects her insurance company to provide her easy-to-use software to complete required tasks.

Challenges

Lola tries to extend the benefits of her yoga & meditation practice into all aspects of her life, but it can be challenging to stay centered when technical problems arise.

Frustrations

Lola's time is valuable, so she gets frustrated when software programs don't work as promised, or when she has to wait on hold to get help from customer service.

Needs

Lola needs an application that makes it easy to complete the dreaded end-of-year audit on her insurance policy premiums.

The initial user research was used to guide the sprint planning process.

Design Sprints focused on 3 additions to the application that would help keep Lola zen while completing the yearly audit for her insurance premiums.
  1. Provide helpful Audit Info upfront
  2. Enhance the Audit Stepper
  3. Add an Audit Summary

Provide helpful Audit Info upfront

Lola needs to have a better understanding of the audit process upfront. She should be able to log into the audit application and be provided helpful information to get her started.

"What accounting information will they ask for? I wonder if I can come back to finish later..."

The application didn't inform users upfront about the audit process.

An audit intro animation was added after login to help answer frequently asked "Getting Started" questions.

Add an Audit Summary

Lola should see a snapshot view of her audit completion before submitting, so she can go back and complete any sections she may have missed.

"Why won't this submit? I don't see anything wrong."

Data collected by the Customer Service Center helped us understand the top reasons for phone calls from customers like Megan.

As a small business owner with variable expenses, Megan needs the flexibility to pay more or less than the amount due on her policy balance.
She also needs the ability to schedule a one-time payment in advance so she doesn't have to worry about making a payment on a specific date.

I created wireframes for a new one-time payment process flow to review with the project team and business stakeholders.

We already had a process for adding a new card or bank account (Design Sprint 1), but brainstorming with wireframes revealed the need to automatically save payment information added during the one-time payment process.
Pay Other and pay amount due options located on the home screen.
Any payment amount can be entered, not only the amount due.
Schedule Payment option is available to set an advanced payment date.
Once the user selects a payment date, they are returned to the previous screen.

Feedback collected from the wireframes was used to create high-fidelity prototypes for development.
