Project Timeline

April 2019

About the Company

EMPLOYERS Insurance Group (NYSE:EIG) is a leading Worker's Compensation Insurance Carrier for small businesses in the the United States.

Interesting Facts

I joined the team after product launch, and spent a majority of my time solving UX issues experienced by real users in production.

The project team used Agile Scrum Methodology through the project lifecyle. I was the sole User Experience Designer, and participated in all scrum ceremonies and workshops.

The project team included 8 team members across 3 U.S. timezones. I worked both remotely and from the company's office in Kansas City, MO.

Lola

The Yoga Studio Owner

Megan owns a trendy clothing boutique with 1 full-time employee and 2 part-time employees.

The boutique is in a prime location, so Megan and her team are always busy with customers in the store. Whenever possible, they grab a few minutes to manage the store's online orders.

Goals

Megan wants to run a successful business, but she also wants to have time for a life outside of work.

Expectations

Megan expects to easily manage her insurance policy from her mobile device, since she rarely has time to work from her desk.

Challenges

Her insurance policy is affordable, but the company doesn't provide easy-to-use tools to help her manage her payments.

Frustrations

Megan gets frustrated when tasks take longer than necessary, or are too difficult to figure out.

"The system doesn't allow me to schedule my payment for later. Can you help me get this done?"

Needs

Megan needs a payment app for easily managing payments and billing for her insurance policy.

Initial user research highlighted features needed to provide value to the customer.

Save a payment method to use later

Megan gets frustrated with unnecessary "busy work", so saving a payment method to use later will prevent her from having to enter the same information more than once.

"I hate it when companies ask me for the same information over and over. Can't they store it for later?"

I conducted a workshop with 3 stakeholders to document the data involved in processing a payment.

We placed each data point into 1 of 3 categories:

  1. Must Have
    Data that is required to process the payment.
  2. Should Have
    Data that is required to process the payment.
  3. Nice to Have
    Bonus data that we'd like to collect.

Must Have

Should Have

Nice to Have

We wanted to make the process easy by reducing the amount of data the customer enters manually.

The Problem

Eliminating data from the Must Have and Should Have categories would increase payment processing fees.

The Solution

Auto-fill the application with billing information from another system to reduce manual work for the user.
User flow for saving a payment method to use later. Auto-filled billing section is displayed in red.

I created low-fidelity prototypes to conduct usability testing with users and get design feedback from business stakeholders.

Make a flexible one-time payment

Megan needs a flexible process for making payments that can't be set up on AutoPay, such as Final Premium Audit or Medical Deductible Payments.

"AutoPay is great for regular monthly payments, but sometimes I still need to quickly make a one-time payment."

Data collected by the Customer Service Center helped us understand the top reasons for phone calls from customers like Megan.

As a small business owner with variable expenses, Megan needs the flexibility to pay more or less than the amount due on her policy balance.
She also needs the ability to schedule a one-time payment in advance so she doesn't have to worry about making a payment on a specific date.

I created wireframes for a new one-time payment process flow to review with the project team and business stakeholders.

We already had a process for adding a new card or bank account (Design Sprint 1), but brainstorming with wireframes revealed the need to automatically save payment information added during the one-time payment process.
Pay Other and pay amount due options located on the home screen.
Any payment amount can be entered, not only the amount due.
Schedule Payment option is available to set an advanced payment date.
Once the user selects a payment date, they are returned to the previous screen.

Feedback collected from the wireframes was used to create high-fidelity prototypes for development.

View upcoming due dates & payment history

Megan needs to easily access the information that helps her manage the payments on her insurance policy.

"I just need the ability to view my past and future payments, and don't ever look at all that other policy stuff."

Initial business requirements asked for all available payment data to be included in the design.

This cluttered the User Interface and wasn't required for users to complete their primary goal of making a payment.
First wireframe showing cluttered UI when displaying all payment data.

Virtual interviews with 4 policyholders helped eliminate data that didn't provide value to the user.

Using a mobile-first design approach, and data collected from user interviews, helped get business stakeholders on board with decluttering the User Interface.
Final prototype showing unnecessary data removed to declutter the UI.
