Premium Audit

Helps customers complete a required audit for premiums paid on their Insurance Policy

About the Project

This portfolio piece is based on a project I worked on for Employers Insurance Group (NYSE:EIG). Employers is a Worker's Compensation Insurance Carrier for small businesses in the United States.

Project Timeline

April 2019

September 2019

Interesting Facts

The project team used Agile Scrum Methodology through the project lifecyle. I was the sole User Experience Designer, and participated in all scrum ceremonies and workshops.

The project team included approximately 10 team members across 3 U.S. timezones. I worked both remotely and from the company's office in Kansas City, MO.

My Role

  • User Research & Usability Testing
  • User Experience & User Interface Design
  • Contributed to HTML & CSS within Angular
  • Created & Maintained a Design System
  • Contributed to a UI Component Library

Lola

The Yoga Studio Owner

Lola owns a yoga and meditation studio with 2 full-time employees and 7 part-time employees.

Lola runs the yoga and meditation studio and teaches several of the classes throughout the day.

Goals

Lola wishes she could teach more classes, instead of spending so much time managing her business.

Expectations

Lola is tech-savvy, but she expects her insurance company to provide her easy-to-use software to manage her policy.

Challenges

Lola tries to extend the benefits of her yoga & meditation practice into all aspects of her life, but it can be challenging to stay centered when technical problems arise.

Frustrations

Lola's time is valuable, so she gets frustrated when software programs don't work as promised, or when she has to wait on hold to get help from customer service.

Needs

Lola needs an application that makes it easy to complete the dreaded end-of-year audit on her insurance policy premiums.

The initial user research was used to guide the sprint planning process.

Design Sprints focused on 3 additions to the application that would help keep Lola zen while completing the yearly audit for her insurance premiums.
  1. Provide helpful Audit Info upfront
  2. Enhance the Audit Stepper
  3. Add an Audit Summary

Provide helpful Audit Info upfront

Lola needs to have a better understanding of the audit process upfront. She should log into the audit application and see helpful information to get her started.

"What accounting information will they ask for? I wonder if I can come back later to finish..."

Lola starts her yearly audit, but there's no information to help her get started.

Interviews with 2 stakeholders helped determine the most frequently asked questions about the audit process, and I designed an audit intro animation to help answer those questions.

Enhance the Audit Stepper

Lola knows the audit process can take a while, but she expects the application to save her work as she goes, show her progress, and let her know if she's made a mistake.

"I'd be really upset if I had to re-do all of this. I really hope it's being saved."

Lola clicks the Save & Verify button, but nothing happens, so she doesn't know if it's safe to go on to the next step.

Virtual usability testing revealed 3 of the 4 policyholders tested were confused about the purpose of the Save & Verify button, and 2 of those policyholders thought it might be broken.
{% include "svg/premiumaudit/save-and-verify.svg" %}
Before Save & Verify button was used at the end of each step for form validation.

During a team brainstorming session, we decided to remove the button and use field-level validation instead.

A green check mark appears to the right of the form field if it's answered correctly, but not until the user clicks away from the field.
{% include "svg/premiumaudit/green-checks.svg" %}
After Save & Verify button was removed and replaced with field-level validation.

Lola sees a group of error messages at the bottom of her screen, but doesn't know how to fix the issues.

Further usability testing revealed all 4 policyholders tested didn't find the error messages useful, because they weren't able to match the message with the form field.
{% include "svg/premiumaudit/errors-at-end.svg" %}
Before All error messages were grouped at the bottom of the screen

We were able to easily solve this problem with the switch to field-level validation.

The error message appears to the right of the form field if a validation error occurs, but not until the user clicks away from the field.
{% include "svg/premiumaudit/errors-at-side.svg" %}
After Error messages were moved to the right of the form field

Lola has a yoga class to teach soon, but she doesn't know when she'll be finished with her audit.

While observing policyholders using the audit application, 2 out of 4 asked "How long is this audit?" about halfway through the audit stepper.

I designed the side navigation to double as a timeline to solve this problem.

1. A progress indicator was added to the top-left corner.
2. Each step in the side navigation menu shows a status.
3. The active step is highlighted.
4. The icon has a white background when the step isn't complete.
{% include "svg/premiumaudit/sidenav.svg" %}
New Side navigation

I also added other helpful info to the audit stepper based on additional data collected during usability testing.

1. The audit due date was added to the header.

2. The list of documents needed was added to the footer.

3. A new FAQ section was also added to the footer.

{% include "svg/premiumaudit/full-mockup.svg" %}
New Header, footer, and side navigation of audit stepper

Add an Audit Summary

Lola should see a snapshot view of her audit completion before submitting, so she can go back to complete any questions she may have missed.

"Why won't this submit? I don't see anything wrong."

Lola completes the final step, but nothing happens when she clicks the submit button.

Inability to submit the audit was the top reason users called the Customer Service Center during the audit process.
{% include "svg/premiumaudit/gray-submit.svg" %}
Before Disabled submit button

The submit button was disabled until all questions in the audit were answered without errors.

Interviews with 2 Customer Service Representatives revealed users found it difficult to find what needed to be fixed, so an Audit Summary was added at the end of the Audit Stepper.
{% include "svg/premiumaudit/userflow2.svg" %}
User flow displays location for new Audit Summary in red.

Lola can see details for each step on the Audit Summary screen, so she knows what needs to be fixed.

Data collected by Customer Service showed after the Audit Summary was added, there was a dramatic decrease in calls from users not able to submit their audit.
{% include "svg/premiumaudit/audit-summary1.svg" %}
New Audit Summary screen

If Lola reviews any step from the Audit Summary, she doesn't have to click through all the steps again to get back.

{% include "svg/premiumaudit/wireflow.svg" %}
Wire flow displays routing back to audit summary.

Yippee! Lola can finally submit her audit now that all steps on the Audit Summary are complete.

{% include "svg/premiumaudit/audit-summary2.svg" %}
New Active submit button on completed audit summary screen
