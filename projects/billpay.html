Bill Pay

Allows customers to easily manage payments and billing for their Insurance Policy

About the Project

This portfolio piece is based on a project I worked on for Employers Insurance Group (NYSE:EIG). Employers is a Worker's Compensation Insurance Carrier for small businesses in the United States.

Project Timeline

September 2019

April 2020

Interesting Facts

The project team used Agile Scrum Methodology through the project lifecyle. I was the sole User Experience Designer, and participated in all scrum ceremonies and workshops.

The project team included approximately 10 team members across 3 U.S. timezones. I worked both remotely and from the company's office in Kansas City, MO.

My Role

  • User Research & Usability Testing
  • User Experience & User Interface Design
  • Contributed to HTML & CSS within Angular v8.0+
  • Created & Maintained a Design System
  • Contributed to a UI Component Library

Megan

The Boutique Owner

Megan owns a trendy clothing boutique with 1 full-time employee and 2 part-time employees.

The boutique is in a prime location, so Megan and her team are always busy with customers in the store. Whenever possible, they grab a few minutes to manage the store's online orders.

Goals

Megan wants to run a successful business, but she also wants to have time for a life outside of work.

Expectations

Megan expects to easily manage her insurance policy from her mobile device, since she rarely has time to work from her desk.

Challenges

Her insurance policy is affordable, but the company doesn't provide easy-to-use tools to help her manage her payments.

Frustrations

Megan gets frustrated when tasks take longer than necessary, or are too difficult to figure out.

"The system doesn't allow me to schedule my payment for later. Can you help me get this done?"

Needs

Megan needs a payment app for easily managing payments and billing for her insurance policy.

{% include "sectionheaders/numberflag2.njk" %}

Initial user research highlighted features needed to provide value to the customer.

I analyzed 138 user comments collected from intercept survey results, conducted 4 in-person interviews with Customer Service Stakeholders, and performed a heuristic evaluation of the legacy application.
Affinity Mapping was used to organize the survey and interview data.
The legacy application didn't provide many options for the customer.
{% include "sectionheaders/numberflag3.njk" %}

The initial user research was used to guide the sprint planning process.

Design Sprints focused on 4 areas that would help Megan easily manage her payments on-the-go.
  1. Save a payment method to use later
  2. Make a flexible one-time payment
  3. Setup AutoPay with a card or bank account
  4. View upcoming due dates and payment history

Save a payment account to use later

Megan gets frustrated with unnecessary "busy work", so saving a payment account to use later prevents her from having to enter the same information more than once.

"I hate it when companies ask me for the same information over and over. Can't they store it for later?"

Megan doesn't recognize the payment accounts she previously saved.

Virtual usability testing revealed 2 of the 3 policyholders tested were not able to easily recognize their saved payment accounts.
{% include "svg/billpay/sprint1MyAccount1.svg" %}
Phase 1 Saved payment accounts were displayed in a text list on the home screen.

I was able to easily solve this problem by visually displaying the payment accounts as images.

{% include "svg/billpay/sprint1MyAccount2.svg" %}
Phase 2 The home screen was redesigned to display saved payment accounts as a card or check.

The interactive prototype shows Megan scrolling through the images of her saved payment accounts.

Phase 3 Saved payment accounts are displayed as images, and are scrollable.

Megan wants to save her new debit card, but wonders if Add Card is for both debit & credit.

Further usability testing revealed all 3 policyholders tested were initially unsure if Add Bank was for adding checking or savings, and if Add Card was for adding debit or credit.
{% include "svg/billpay/sprint1MyAccount3.svg" %}
Phase 1 Add Bank and Add Card buttons were displayed on the home screen.

I was able to make the process more intuitive by replacing the buttons with an icon.

{% include "svg/billpay/sprint1MyAccount4.svg" %}
Phase 2 The Add Bank and Add Card buttons were consolidated into an Add icon.

The interactive prototype shows Megan starting the process of saving her debit card.

Phase 3 The Add icon is used to add a new payment account.

Megan gets annoyed entering a bunch of information the company already has stored.

During virtual usability testing, 3 of the 4 policyholders tested commented on the amount of data they had to manually enter.
{% include "svg/billpay/sprint1PaymentInfo1.svg" %}
Phase 1 The initial design included all form fields that were present in the legacy application.

I was able to make the process easier by reducing manual data entry.

Interviews with 2 stakeholders revealed eliminating too much data would increase the company's payment processing fees, so we auto-filled the application with policy info from another system instead.
{% include "svg/billpay/sprint1PaymentInfo2.svg" %}
Phase 2 Policy information from another system was used to auto-fill the Billing Information section.

The final interactive prototype shows Megan saving her debit card to use later.

Phase 3 It's simple to add a new account, and edit the billing info if needed.

Make a flexible one-time payment

As a small business owner with variable expenses, Megan needs the flexibility to pay more or less than the amount due and schedule a one-time payment in advance.

"It seems like all my bills are due at once, so I need an insurance company that lets me make flexible payments."

Megan finds the two buttons for making a payment confusing.

Virtual usability testing revealed 1 of the 3 policyholders tested thought the Pay Other button was used to pay on a second policy.
{% include "svg/billpay/sprint2PaymentButton1.svg" %}
Phase 1 One button was for paying the amount due, and the other was for entering a different amount.

The two buttons were consolidated into a Make a Payment button.

This finding was likely unique to a small group of customers holding multiple policies, but it was a good opportunity to improve the design.
{% include "svg/billpay/sprint2PaymentButton2.svg" %}
Phase 2 The user is asked about the payment amount after clicking the Make a Payment button.

The interactive prototype shows Megan making a payment for less than the amount due.

Phase 3 A payment can be made for any amount.

Megan is in the process of making a payment, but the payment account she wants to use isn't saved yet.

Usability testing found a flaw in the design where a payment account had to be saved before making a payment.
{% include "svg/billpay/sprint2AddNew1.svg" %}
Phase 1 Only saved payment accounts could be used to make payments.

I was able to easily solve this problem by inserting a button for adding a new account while making a payment.

{% include "svg/billpay/sprint2AddNew2.svg" %}
Phase 2 The Add New button allowed the user to enter a new account without starting over.

The final interactive prototype shows Megan adding a new payment account while making a payment.

Phase 3 A new card is added while making a payment.

Setup AutoPay with a card or bank account

Megan is busy running her business, so she needs the ability to setup AutoPay for her regular monthly payments.

"I want to set it and forget it so I don't have to worry about missing a payment due date again."

Megan decides she wants to setup AutoPay while making a payment, but doesn't see it as an option.

Virtual usability testing revealed policyholders were likely to look for AutoPay options while making a one-time payment.
{% include "svg/billpay/sprint3AutoPay1.svg" %}
Phase 1 There was no option to setup AutoPay while making a payment.

I was able to easily solve this problem by adding a link for AutoPay to the payment screen.

{% include "svg/billpay/sprint3AutoPay2.svg" %}
Phase 2 A link to set up AutoPay is now part of the one-time payment process.

The interactive prototype shows Megan using the link to setup AutoPay instead of making a one-time payment.

Phase 3 AutoPay is setup from the payment screen.

Megan knows she setup AutoPay the last time she logged in, but can't find the info anywhere.

{% include "svg/billpay/sprint3AutoPay3.svg" %}
Phase 1 The home screen didn't indicate that AutoPay was enabled.

Details were added to help policyholders easily setup and manage AutoPay at-a-glance.

{% include "svg/billpay/sprint3AutoPay4.svg" %}
Phase 2 Now it's clear when AutoPay is enabled, and if the next payment will be automatically deducted.

The interactive prototype shows Megan managing her AutoPay from the home screen.

Phase 3 AutoPay information is easily managed, and available at-a-glance from the home screen.

View upcoming due dates & payment history

Megan needs to easily access the information that helps her manage the payments on her insurance policy.

"I just need the ability to view my past and future payments, and don't ever look at all that other policy stuff."

The legacy application displayed all available policy information.

This was included in the initial wireframe, but it cluttered the UI, and made understanding the data difficult.
{% include "svg/billpay/sprint4PaymentInfo1.svg" %}
Phase 1 The UI was cluttered with all the info from the legacy system.

Any data not important to the user was removed to declutter the UI.

Interviews with 3 policyholders helped me filter out unnecessary information that doesn't help the user accomplish their primary goal of making a payment.
{% include "svg/billpay/sprint4PaymentInfo2.svg" %}
Phase 2 Removing unnecessary data helped clean up the UI.

The interactive prototype shows Megan using the main menu to access upcoming due dates and payment history.

Phase 3 The clean UI makes understanding payment and billing info easy.

Future plans include:

  1. Monitor customer service requests, Google Analytics, user surveys, etc. for UX issues in production.
  2. Ability to pay with Apple Pay, Samsung Pay, PayPal, etc.
  3. Integrate with popular bank applications to further reduce manual data entry.
  4. Receive email and text alerts for upcoming payments.
