Bill Pay

Allows customers to easily manage payments and billing for their Insurance Policy

About the Project

This portfolio piece is based on a project I worked on for Employers Insurance Group (NYSE:EIG). Employers is a Worker's Compensation Insurance Carrier for small businesses in the United States.

Project Timeline

September 2019

April 2020

Interesting Facts

The project team used Agile Scrum Methodology through the project lifecyle. I was the sole User Experience Designer, and participated in all scrum ceremonies and workshops.

The project team included approximately 10 team members across 3 U.S. timezones. I worked both remotely and from the company's office in Kansas City, MO.

My Role

  • Contributed to HTML & CSS within Angular v8.0+

Megan

The Boutique Owner

Megan owns a trendy clothing boutique with 1 full-time employee and 2 part-time employees.

The boutique is in a prime location, so Megan and her team are always busy with customers in the store. Whenever possible, they grab a few minutes to manage the store's online orders.

Goals

Megan wants to run a successful business, but she also wants to have time for a life outside of work.

Expectations

Megan expects to easily manage her insurance policy from her mobile device, since she rarely has time to work from her desk.

Challenges

Her insurance policy is affordable, but the company doesn't provide easy-to-use tools to help her manage her payments.

Frustrations

Megan gets frustrated when tasks take longer than necessary, or are too difficult to figure out.

"The system doesn't allow me to schedule my payment for later. Can you help me get this done?"

Needs

Megan needs a payment app for easily managing payments and billing for her insurance policy.

Initial user research highlighted features needed to provide value to the customer.

I analyzed 138 user comments collected from intercept survey results, conducted 4 in-person interviews with Customer Service Stakeholders, and performed a heuristic evaluation of the legacy application.
Affinity Mapping was used to organize the survey and interview data.
The legacy application didn't provide many options for the customer.
{% include "sectionheaders/numberflag3.njk" %}

The initial user research was used to guide the sprint planning process.

Design Sprints focused on 4 areas that would help Megan easily manage her payments on-the-go.
  1. Save a payment method to use later
  2. Make a flexible one-time payment
  3. Setup AutoPay with a card or bank account
  4. View upcoming due dates and payment history

Save a payment account to use later

Megan gets frustrated with unnecessary "busy work", so saving a payment account to use later will prevent her from having to enter the same information more than once.

"I hate it when companies ask me for the same information over and over. Can't they store it for later?"

Megan dreads paying her bill each month, because it takes too much time to enter all the payment info.

One of the top complaints from customers about the legacy system was the amount of time it took to make a simple payment.

I conducted a workshop with 3 stakeholders to document the data involved in processing a payment.

We placed each data point into 1 of 3 categories:

  1. Must Have
    Data that is required to process the payment.
  2. Should Have
    Data that we should have to process the payment.
  3. Nice to Have
    Bonus data that we'd like to collect.

Must Have

Should Have

Nice to Have

We wanted to make the process easy by reducing the amount of data the customer enters manually.

The Problem

Eliminating data from the Must Have and Should Have categories would increase payment processing fees.

The Solution

Auto-fill the application with billing information from another system to reduce manual work for the user.

Make a flexible one-time payment

Megan is a small business owner with variable expenses. She needs to make a partial payment now, and pay the rest in two weeks.

"It seems like all my bills are due at once, so I need an insurance company that lets me make flexible payments."

Data collected by the Customer Service Center helped us understand the top reasons for phone calls from customers like Megan.

As a small business owner with variable expenses, Megan needs the flexibility to pay more or less than the amount due on her policy balance.
She also needs the ability to schedule a one-time payment in advance so she doesn't have to worry about making a payment on a specific date.

Setup AutoPay with a card or bank account

Megan is busy running her business, so she needs the ability to setup AutoPay for her regular monthly payments.

"I want to set it and forget it so I don't have to worry about missing a payment due date again."

{% include "sectionheaders/numberflag7.njk" %}

View upcoming due dates & payment history

Megan needs to easily access the information that helps her manage the payments on her insurance policy.

"I just need the ability to view my past and future payments, and don't ever look at all that other policy stuff."

Future plans include:

  1. Monitor customer service requests, Google Analytics, user surveys, etc. for UX issues in production.
  2. Ability to pay with Apple Pay, Samsung Pay, PayPal, etc.
  3. Integrate with popular bank applications to further reduce manual data entry.
  4. Receive email and text alerts for upcoming payments.
